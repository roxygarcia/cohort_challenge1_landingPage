UX VS UI

What is UX Design?

The user's overall experience while interacting with your website.

Usability and Functionality

It's the sequence of actions the user will take as they interact with the interface. How easy or difficult is the webpage to navigate. Is it intuitive or confusing and clunky? Is it unique and inviting? Is it fun?

What is UI Design?

The user interface design. The look and feel of your website.

Flow and Structure

This is the architecture. The layout and placement of all the components or elements that will become part of your site. What visual elements will you be using? How will you present them?

Why care about UX and UI you ask?

1. There are things that never change

Whether it be 100 years from now or 100 years back in history, pictures can speak a thousand words, logos can make your brand/company recognized all over the world, and a great looking UI with an easy to navigate UX can make your company.

2. Concise and to the point make a world of a difference

A landing page that keeps functionality and efficiency constantly in mind keeps prospecive clients engaged and allows them the ability to find what they are looking for quickly. No need to navigate through page after page or paragraph after paragraph to find the answer they seek.

3. Pleasing to the eye

Whether we are referring to the past, present, or future there will always be customers who judge a book by its cover. If a webpage is easy to use but hard to look at the greater the chance the user will click the dreaded "back" button and search someplace else. This can easily be fixed by making your UI design a priority.
